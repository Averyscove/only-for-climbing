10 NEW DIRECTIONS · CONCEPT-LED

Resetting. Forget the badge-with-monkey trope. These ten ideas each commit to a single concept — the mark *means* something specific to climbers — and lean on type, negative space, gesture, and texture rather than literal mascots. Each is sized to work as a tiny app icon, a chalk-bag screenprint, and a 12-foot gym mural.

Premise

Most climbing brands look the same: round stamp + mountain + serif. We avoid all three.

Test

Could a climber, walking past the gym at night, recognize this from 30 ft? Then read it from 6 inches?

Constraint

Two colors max per mark. Texture earned, not applied. Every choice answerable with "because climbing."

11

Chalk Handprint — The Last Mark Left

NEGATIVE SPACE · GESTURAL · UNIQUE TO CLIMBING
DIRECTION 11
Chalk Handprint
"The handprint is the climber's signature. It belongs to no one else."

Owns a gesture that's instantly, exclusively climbing. The wordmark lives inside the print, not next to it — so the brand IS the trace you leave on the rock. Photographs beautifully on dark walls.

12

Crimped C — Type IS the Action

LIGATURE · LITERAL · CLEVER
DIRECTION 12
Crimped C
"Climbers know: the C isn't a letter, it's a hold."

The "C" of CLIMBING becomes a crimp edge with fingers physically gripping it. Insider's mark — non-climbers see clean type, climbers see what they do every day. Highest-recall potential of the set.

13

Grade Stack — V0 to V17

SYSTEMATIC · CONCEPTUAL · ZERO LITERAL IMAGERY
DIRECTION 13
Grade Stack
"The grade scale itself becomes our identity."

Brand-as-data-visualization. The mark literally is the V-scale, with V7-V9 highlighted in send-orange to mark the "social middle" where most users live. Endlessly remixable for events: highlight V12 for a comp poster, V0 for beginner night.

14

Rock Stamp — Punched Through Texture

PHOTOGRAPHIC TEXTURE · STAMPED · RAW
DIRECTION 14
Rock Stamp
"Looks chalked onto a real wall in a real gym."

Most photographic of the set. The wordmark feels painted onto granite, not designed. Trades vector cleanliness for atmospheric authenticity — best on murals, posters, hoodies. Reduce to a 2-color stamp for app icon.

15

Route Flag — Letters on a Line

BANNER · KINETIC · COMPOSITION-DRIVEN
DIRECTION 15
Route Flag
"The wordmark itself has to be climbed."

Letters as fixed-line flags following an upward diagonal. Has motion built in. Most "narrative" of the set — works exceptionally for splash screens, animations, and onboarding (letters could appear one at a time as you scroll up).

16

Compression — Squeezed Between Walls

SPATIAL METAPHOR · TENSION · ARCHITECTURAL
DIRECTION 16
Compression
"Brand under pressure. Just like every climb."

The wordmark is condensed (vertically scaled 1.5x) as if literally squeezed between two rock walls — a chimney climb. Tension and verticality built into the lockup. A bold, dramatic option for hero moments — splash screens, posters, brand films.

17

Holds Typography — Letters as Holds

FONT-AS-SYSTEM · PLAYFUL · OWNABLE
DIRECTION 17
Holds Typography
"The font is the wall. Every letter is something you'd grab."

Each glyph in "ALL FOR" is a different style of climbing hold (sloper, jug, pinch, pocket, crimp, sloper-combo). Becomes a full typeface system — extend to A–Z and you can spell anything in climbing-language. Most expansive long-term identity.

18

Figure-8 Knot — Tied to the Brand

SAFETY · CONTINUITY · LINEWORK
DIRECTION 18
Figure-8 Knot
"The first thing you learn. The thing that keeps you alive."

Figure-8 is the universal climber's knot — instantly recognizable to anyone who's tied in. Acts as both a literal mark and a metaphor for community ("we're tied together"). Animates beautifully — the knot can self-tie on splash.

19

Athletic Tape — Wrapped Mark

TEXTURE · ATHLETIC · LO-FI
DIRECTION 19
Athletic Tape
"The tape on your fingers IS the brand language."

Hand-finger tape strips literally wrap a giant "A" — every climber recognizes the texture instantly. The brand details (year, motto) live ON the tape. Endlessly variable: each user-shareable card could re-wrap differently.

20

SEND IT — Vernacular Mark

SLANG · COMIC · BIG-MOOD
DIRECTION 20
SEND IT
"Make the slang the logo. The product name is the supporting cast."

Most aggressive bet of the set: "SEND" becomes the primary recognizable mark, while "all for climbing" plays the supporting role. Trades discoverability for cultural ownership — when a climber sees this, they instantly belong. Strong on social, posters, gym murals.
